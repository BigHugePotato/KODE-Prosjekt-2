Collatz Conjecture

Welcome to our Collatz Conjecture exploration page!

Here, we delve into one of mathematics' most intriguing puzzles. The Collatz Conjecture is a simple yet unsolved problem in number theory.


It starts with any positive integer, (n), and follows a straightforward rule: if (n) is even, divide it by 2; if (n) is odd, multiply it by 3 and add 1.

Repeat these steps with each new value. The conjecture suggests that no matter the starting number, this sequence will always reach 1.

While it sounds easy, the Collatz Conjecture has evaded proof or disproof for years, presenting a fascinating challenge for mathematicians and enthusiasts alike.

It serves as a perfect example of how a simple mathematical formula can produce complex and unpredictable results.
